Streams & Fields

In India, you generally get to choose between 3 streams in classes 11th & 12th, namely Science, Commerce, and Humanities (or Arts). Each stream is unique and has its own kind of challenges for a student. It's not necessary that a student great at Math can graciously pass Science, or a language loving student can score high in Arts.

Every student has its own interest and capabilities and should not be compared with another on the basis of marks. Also, these streams do not enable you to judge a student’s IQ. Till a few years back, the calibre of a student was measured on the basis of the stream s/he gets (because of course, in India, the marking scheme divisions set the eligibility of the streams.) But now, people have started encouraging all streams equally, which is a wonderful change.

We all are looking forward to the changes from The National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020), which was approved by the Union Cabinet of India on 29 July 2020 to come into motion, as it will remove the general distinction between streams and enable a student to take up all the subjects of his/her choice.

Science Stream

Science Stream in India is mainly divided into 2 sub-categories, which are- Medical and Non-Medical. Medical consists of Physics, Chemistry, Biology whereas, Non-Medical includes Physics, Chemistry, Maths. One can opt for Computers with Non-Medical and Maths with Medical as well.

The stream of Science can make you eligible for Graduate courses such as Engineering, Honors in any of the Science subjects, all sorts of Medical courses, NDA, defence courses, etc. It is also believed that one can change their lines if s/he had Science.

Science in general broadens the logical thinking of a student, increases the ways in which one can see a problem, gives you a scientific touch in theories, and possibly sharpens your brain.

Humanities Stream

Humanities, or generally known as Arts stream consists of subjects like Visual and Performing Arts, Literary Arts, History, Geography, Political Science. Some institutions provide an option to students to opt for Maths or Information Practices with an Arts stream.

The stream of Humanities enables you to pursue courses like Language Specialization, Journalism, Psychology, Travel & Tourism, Arts/Fine Arts, Design, Hotel Management, etc. This stream tests your creative side, enables you to learn new things everyday, go into vast details of the subjects and make you a better person.

Commerce Stream

Commerce stream can be taken with or without Maths. Its main subjects are Accountancy, Business Studies and Economics. Many institutions also provide an option to the student to opt for Information Practices as an extra subject with Commerce.

The stream of Commerce can lead you to Honors in any of the Commerce subjects, management courses, Business related courses, Banking, CA, etc.

Commerce makes your brain calculate fast, it gives you an ability to think how a business actually works, and also gives you knowledge about how the exchange of goods and services is done from producer to final consumer, basically how a business or management works.

How To Apply To Colleges?

Delhi University

Delhi University is a national University, controlled by the Central Government, based in Delhi. It has 2 main campuses- North Campus and South Campus at the North and South or Delhi, respectively. Along with this, it has colleges scattered around Delhi & NCR region which are non-campus colleges. Campus life in DU is considered fun and enjoyable.

Admission in Delhi University Colleges is mainly merit-based and a few courses consider a fraction of merit and a fraction of entrance exams. Admission procedure is Online, where an aspirant can apply, submit his/her details, check eligibility, and much more.

IITD

IIT is the biggest name for Engineering in India. The Delhi Campus, located in Hauz Khas is huge and the campus life is vast. They say it’s hard to get into an IIT, but it’s harder to survive it till the end. Enormous competition and cut-throat eligibility is filled with both- anxiety and excitement.

Admission in IIT, all over the country takes place through the Join Entrance Examination.

JNU

Jawaharlal Nehru University is a public central university. It was established in 1969 and named after Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister. The university is known for leading faculties and research emphasis on liberal arts and applied sciences. It comes under top 10 universities in India.

Admission in JNU takes place in a different manner by the entrance exams conducted by the institution through JNUEE & JNU CEEB further followed by a viva voce examination after which students are selected through a unique selection process.

AIIMS

All India Institute of Medical Sciences is a medical school, hospital and public medical research university. The institute is ranked 1 in India by the National Institutional Ranking Framework, ranked 1 in South Asia.AIIMS (New Delhi) is governed by the All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956. Getting a seat here is a very difficult job but staying here needs a large amount of dedication too.

Admission in AIIMS takes place by the scores in entrance examinations NEET and other examinations conducted by AIIMS itself. Candidates need to have a very good rank AIR in NEET in order to take admission here in MBBS, MD etc courses.

NLU

National Law University is a National University, offering courses at the undergraduate and postgraduate levels. Situated in Sector-14, Dwarka, New Delhi. NLUD is one of the national law schools in India built on the five-year law degree model.

>Here, admissions take place by All India Law Entrance Test (AILET), a test conducted every year by the university for various undergraduates programs.

GGSIPU

Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University, commonly known as IP University is controlled by the State Government and has scattered colleges all over Delhi & NCR region. Many colleges are also affiliated to this University.

Admission in GGSIPU is wholly entrance based. A student has to apply online and pay a fee for entrance examinations to sit in the examinations (Every course has a minimum eligibility of merit to let a student apply). Then the cut-offs are released and the admissions take place. A very unique round of spot filling also takes place at the end, in order to fill up all the empty seats in the colleges.

IGNOU

Indira Gandhi National Open University known as IGNOU, is a Central University located at Maidan Garhi, named after former Prime Minister of India, Indira Gandhi. It was founded to serve the Indian population by means of distance and open education, providing quality higher education opportunities to all segments of society.

Admission to IGNOU takes place by filling online application forms and submitting their fees. It conducts entrance exams for some courses two times a year, but mainly admission takes place through merit list.

Jamia Milia Islamia

Jamia Millia Islamia is a Central University located in Jamia Nagar, New Delhi. This University has special reservation policies for Muslim candidates and offers a wide range of courses.

Admission to Jamia takes place through entrance examinations, and for engineering courses, JEE score/rank is considered. There is an eligibility criteria for an aspirant to apply for the entrance exams, which is based on the merit of Class 12th.

DTU

Delhi Technical University formerly known as the Delhi College of Engineering (DCE) is a state university. It was established in 1941 as Delhi Polytechnic. In 2009, the college was given university status, thus changing its name to Delhi Technological University. It is situated in Rohini and has a campus providing all the facilities and well-connected to the roads.

The admission process here completely depends on various entrance exams like JEE, GATE, CAT etc and if two or more students score then the selection will be done on merit-based.

IIMC

Indian Institute of Mass Communication is an Indian media centre of learning, funded and promoted by the Government of India.The IIMC is an autonomous society under the Ministry of Information and Broadcasting. IIMC teaches a variety of disciplines including print journalism, photojournalism, radio journalism, television journalism, development communication, communication research, advertising and public relations. The institute was basically set up to provide training to the officers of the Indian Information Service.

Admissions to these courses is taken through All India Entrance Examination (IIMC Entrance Exam) followed by Group Discussions and Interview.

Jamia Hamdard

Jamia Hamdard is an institute of higher education deemed to be university located. It is a government-funded university. This university offers a multitude of graduate programmes in Modern Medicine, Information Technology, Computer Applications, Business Management, Physiotherapy and Occupational Therapy. Undergraduate programmes in Physiotherapy and Occupational Therapy and Post Graduate Diploma in Preventive Cardiology.

Admission here is done by the scores of various entrance examinations like NEET(for MBBS, MD), JEE and entrance conducted by the university itself.
